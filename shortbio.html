Sydney Benton | Environemtal Science Student

I am undergraduate in environmental science at the University of Iowa, with a focus in learning GIS, studying hydrogeologic phenomena in the field, collecting ecological data, and learning the fundamentals of sustainable resource management. My recent coursework includes spatial distribution analysis and habitat suitability modeling using GIS. I have experience in hydrogeologic research, working with well-head data, slug tests, and borehole cores to calculate groundwater characteristics. Additionally, I have experience testing groundwater samples and utilizing the results for project write-ups. My contributions to the Office of Undergraduate Research program at Iowa centered on developing a method for collecting and analyzing soil health data in Iowa prairies. I've also collaborated on ecological projects at the Ashton Prairie Living Laboratory, including research on entomological and botanical influences in Iowa prairies.
